Wali : Hands-Free Baby Gate

2.009 Product

  • Date: Fall 2014

Wali is a baby gate with sensors to detect whether an object that is approaching the gate is an adult or not, then safely unlocks to protect children, but allow ease of use by adults. I mainly worked on the electronics and sensors but as a team of 24 students and several mentors, we all worked together to develop the product during the semester. Skyline Yoyos

2.008 Project

  • Date: Fall 2014

Click here to see the team's blog for a detailed explanation of the project. A team of 6 students made 50 yoyos to tight tolerances. There were 4 injection molded parts and 1 thermoformed part which snap-fit together in each yoyo. Inspired by the Brass Rat, on one side is the Boston skyline; the Cambridge skyline is on the other. Each skyline rotates on a bearing so it can stay still even as the yoyo spins.

Quadrotor Safety System

Robustness to Communication Dropouts

  • Date: Spring 2016

During normal autonomous flight, there are bound to be communication issues. Sometimes it is just a few packets being dropped, and other times we observed several tenths of a second of wifi dropout.

In this system, vehicles constantly monitor the health of their communication with an offboard computer. If the dropout is very short, it is ignored. If it is slightly longer, the vehicle begins to execute a maneuver to zero its velocities. If the dropout is still happening at this point, the vehicle performs an open loop landing.

This new ability to handle the full spectrum of communication issues allows experiments and demonstrations to last longer and be more reliable. Future work in this project will incorporate on-board sensing to estimate altitude and velocity and close the loop on the safety maneuvers.
